About Us

Institute of Asian Studies

The Institute of Asian Studies (IAS, Belgrade, Serbia) is a think-tank engaged in research, educational, publishing, and consulting activities with the aim of spreading knowledge about Asia in our country and region and thereby contributing to cooperation between countries in Central and Southeastern Europe and Asian countries. Its geographical focus is on the People's Republic of China, Japan, India, Republic of Korea, DPR Korea, Russian Federation, Central Asian and Caspian states, Turkey, ASEAN countries, Australia, and New Zealand.

IAS conducts research on internal politics, economics, and social development, foreign policy, international economic relations of each of the mentioned countries, regional economic integrations, political, security, infrastructure, interpersonal, and other forms of cooperation, issues, and structures that define the present of Asia but also determine its future development.

IAS publishes the annual journal "Asian Annals" and focuses on building a collaborative network with partner institutions from Asia, as well as other parts of the world.

Our Team

Research Team

Prof. Dr. Dragana Mitrović
Founder and President of IAS

Dr. Dragana Mitrović is a full professor at the Faculty of Political Sciences, University of Belgrade, founder, and president of the Institute of Asian Studies (www.ias.rs). She is a leading expert in economics, politics, international relations, and security of contemporary China and East Asia in our region.

Dr. Frank Don't
Ph.D. in History, Yale University, USA, IAS Member

Frank Don't is a senior lecturer in history at the Institute of Asian Studies, University of Brunei Darussalam. He also teaches in Indonesia and in the broader Malay cultural area in Southeast Asia. As a 20th-century history researcher, he is particularly focused on studying the reactions of local leaders.

Dr. Jagannath Panda
IAS Member

Dr. Jagannath Panda is the head of the Stockholm Centre for South Asian and Indo-Pacific Affairs (SCSA-IPA) at the Institute for Security and Development Policy (ISDP), Sweden. He is also the Director for Research Cooperation in Europe and Asia at YCAPS and a scientific advisor at The Hague Centre for Strategic Studies (HCSS).

John Nicotera
Law Graduate, IAS Member

Mr. Nicotera served at the United Nations Office on Drugs and Crime (UNODC) from 1997 to 2012. He held various positions at the UN headquarters in Vienna, including work in the United Nations Crime Prevention and Criminal Justice Commission.
