About Us

Institute of Asian Studies

The Institute of Asian Studies (IAS, Belgrade, Serbia) is a think-tank engaged in research, educational, publishing, and consulting activities with the aim of spreading knowledge about Asia in our country and region and thereby contributing to cooperation between countries in Central and Southeastern Europe and Asian countries. Its geographical focus is on the People's Republic of China, Japan, India, Republic of Korea, DPR Korea, Russian Federation, Central Asian and Caspian states, Turkey, ASEAN countries, Australia, and New Zealand.

IAS conducts research on internal politics, economics, and social development, foreign policy, international economic relations of each of the mentioned countries, regional economic integrations, political, security, infrastructure, interpersonal, and other forms of cooperation, issues, and structures that define the present of Asia but also determine its future development.

IAS publishes the annual journal "Asian Annals" and focuses on building a collaborative network with partner institutions from Asia, as well as other parts of the world.

Our Team

Research Team

Prof. Dr. Dragana Mitrović
Founder and President of IAS

Prof. Dr. Dragana Mitrović, a regular professor at the Faculty of Political Sciences, University of Belgrade, founder and president of the Institute for Asian Studies (www.ias.rs), is a prominent expert in the fields of economy, politics, international relations, and security of contemporary China and East Asia in our region.

Dr. Frank Dhont
PhD in History, Yale University, USA, IAS Member

Dr. Frank Dhont holds a doctorate in history from Yale University, USA, and is a member of the Institute for Asian Studies. He is a Senior Lecturer in History at the Institute of Asian Studies, University of Brunei Darussalam. He also teaches in Indonesia and the wider Malay cultural area in Southeast Asia. As a researcher in 20th-century history, he is particularly focused on studying the reactions of local leaders and populations to the Japanese occupation of the Dutch East Indies and the Malayan world during World War II.

Dr. Jagannath Panda
IAS Member

Dr. Jagannath Panda is the head of the Stockholm Centre for South Asian and Indo-Pacific Affairs (SCSA-IPA) at the Institute for Security and Development Policy (ISDP), Sweden. He also serves as the Director for Research Cooperation on Europe and Asia at YCAPS and is a Senior Fellow at The Hague Centre for Strategic Studies (HCSS).

John Nikotera
Law Graduate, IAS Member

Mr. Nikotera served in the United Nations Office on Drugs and Crime (UNODC) from 1997 to 2012. He held various positions at the UN headquarters in Vienna, including working in the United Nations Commission on Crime Prevention and Criminal Justice.

Badar Alam Iqbal
Emeritus Professor, IAS Member

Emeritus Professor Badar Alam Iqbal; Ph.D. & DBA; M.Com; DSW; B.A. [Hons] Economics had been a DAAD Fellow at the German Institute of Economic Research in Berlin, the Kiel Institute of World Economics in Kiel, and the South Asia Institute at the University of Heidelberg. He has also been associated with the Ford Foundation's American Research Center.

Srdjan Perišić
PhD in Political Science, IAS Member

Dr. Srdjan Perišić graduated from the Military Academy in Belgrade. He holds two master's degrees from the Faculty of Political Sciences in Belgrade. His first master's was in Political Theory and Methodology of Political Science, with a thesis on Liberalism as a Constraint of Modern Democracy.

Ivan Zaric
PhD in Political Science, IAS Member

Dr. Ivan Zaric graduated from the Military Academy in Belgrade, Department of Naval Warfare. He obtained his master's degree in International Relations from the Faculty of Political Sciences in Belgrade, specializing in Geopolitics and Geostrategy.

Dr. Dragan Trailović
Specialist in Asian Regional Studies, Executive Director of IAS

Dr. Dragan Trailović graduated from the Faculty of Political Sciences, University of Belgrade, in 2008, specializing in International Relations. He completed his postgraduate studies in Asian Regional Studies at the same faculty.

Ambassador Teruhiko Shinada
Special Advisor to IAS

Ambassador Teruhiko Shinada served in Japan's diplomatic service for over 40 years. Education: Keio University, Japan, Economics Faculty. As a diplomat, he held numerous leadership positions, including serving at the Embassy of Japan in Belgrade (1980-86, 1992-95, 2006-10), the Office of the High Representative in Sarajevo (1996), the Permanent Mission of Japan to the United Nations in New York (1997-98), and the Embassy of Japan in Vienna (1999-2001).
